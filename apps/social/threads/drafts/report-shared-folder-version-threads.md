# Threads draft v2 — "a colleague saved over my own report"

## Post 1 (hook)

I sat down to finish a report this week.

The plan was easy: open the version I built 3 months ago, reuse my own template, keep going.

Then I opened the shared folder.
A colleague had saved over my file — and I couldn't even remember what my version looked like anymore. 🧵

## Post 2 (the pain / reframe)

Nobody warns you about this with shared folders:

your file isn't really *yours*. People move it, rename it, "tidy it up," save on top of it.

Then months later you go back to build on your own work — and the version in your head is just gone.

It's not one dramatic delete. It's your own history quietly disappearing.

## Post 3 (soft Keeply + backlink)

What I actually needed: a version history that keeps every past version of my work and never expires — even when the file lives in a shared folder.

So I could just open "my report, 3 months ago" and carry on, template and all.

That's what Keeply does, for teams who live in shared drives.

結果打開共用資料夾——
我的檔案被同事存上去蓋掉了。而我連自己那版長什麼樣，都想不起來了。🧵

## 串 2（痛點 / 反轉）

共用資料夾這件事，沒人會先提醒你：

你的檔案其實不是你的。有人搬它、改名、「順手整理」、直接存上去蓋過。

等你過幾個月想接著做自己的東西——記憶裡那一版，就這樣不見了。

它不是「啪一聲被刪掉」，是你自己的版本，一點一點被磨掉。

## 串 3（軟提 Keeply + backlink）

我真正需要的，是一份不會過期的版本紀錄——就算檔案放在共用資料夾，每一個過去的版本都還留著。

這樣我就能直接打開「我三個月前那版報告」，連範本一起接著做。

這就是 Keeply 在做的事，給每天在共用碟裡工作的團隊。
